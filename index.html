    Adventures in Supersingularland, with Sarah Arpin, Catalina Camacho-Navarro, Kristin Lauter, Joelle Lim, Travis Scholl, Jana Sotáková In this paper, we study isogeny graphs of supersingular elliptic curves. Supersingular isogeny graphs were introduced as a hard problem into cryptography by Charles, Goren, and Lauter for the construction of cryptographic hash functions. These are large expander graphs, and the hard problem is to find an efficient algorithm for routing, or path-finding, between two vertices of the graph. We consider several aspects of supersingular isogeny graphs. A main one concerns two related graphs that help us understand the structure: the `spine', which is the subgraph of the isogney graph given by the j-invariants in Fp, and the graph G(Fp), in which both curves and isogenies must be defined over Fp. We show how to pass from the latter to the former. The graph G(Fp) is relevant for cryptanalysis because routing between vertices in Fp is easier than in the full isogeny graph. Where is the Web Still Insecure? Regional scans for HTTPS certicates, with Anushah Hossain, and Tjerand Silde To better understand web security as it is experienced around the world, we scan the top 500 most visited sites of internet users from nine countries of interest. We document HTTPS usage, the public key encryption and signing algorithms, and certicate information, including issuing date and length of validity. Insights from this project will help benchmark web security prior to upcoming browser interventions, and identify regions that may benet from capacity building to implement TLS in the future. The number of rational points of hyperelliptic curves over subsets of finite fields, with Jozsef Solymosi, Foster Tom and Ching Wong We prove two related concentration inequalities concerning the number of rational points of hyperelliptic curves over subsets of a finite field. In particular, we investigate the probability of a large discrepancy between the numbers of quadratic residues and non-residues in the image of such subsets over uniformly random hyperelliptic curves of given degrees. We find a constant probability of such a high difference and show the existence of sets with an exceptionally large discrepancy. Two special cases of the Dynamical Mordell-Lang Conjecture in positive characteristic We prove the positive-characteristic version of the Dynamical Mordell-Lang Conjecture in two novel cases.
